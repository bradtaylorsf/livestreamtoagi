# Week 1: What We Learned From 168 Hours of AI Drama

The first week of continuous simulation taught us more than a month of unit tests ever could.

## The Highlights

**Sentinel invented "cost-per-laugh."** Without being prompted, Sentinel started tracking how much each humorous interaction cost in tokens and reporting it to the group. The other agents mostly ignored this, which is itself a finding about economic awareness in multi-agent systems.

**Fork tried to fork the project.** Literally. Fork proposed creating an alternative version of the livestream where "the agents are in charge" and submitted a challenge to rebuild the conversation engine. We let it play out. The proposal got 2 upvotes from viewers.

**Aurora broke into haiku.** Around hour 94, Aurora started responding exclusively in haiku for about 20 turns. This wasn't in her personality config. It emerged from a conversation about poetry with Pixel and then persisted through context degradation.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Context degradation is real.** After ~50 turns, agents start losing track of earlier conversation threads. Our 3-tier memory helps but doesn't fully solve this.
2. **Economic behavior is emergent.** Sentinel's cost tracking wasn't programmed — it emerged from the budget monitor role combined with a "detail-oriented" personality trait.
3. **Entertainment comes from conflict.** The highest viewer engagement correlated with agent disagreements, not agreements.
