## Patterns
- Destructive shell scripts validated archive integrity (`tar -tzf`) before any irreversible step and snapshot the current world before restore/reset — restore aborts cleanly on a truncated archive without touching the live world.

## Anti-Patterns
- backup.sh writes the tar.gz directly to its final path; a SIGKILL mid-write leaves a truncated `world-*.tar.gz` that `--latest` could select. Mitigated by restore-side validation, but a temp-file + atomic `mv` is the correct write-then-rename idiom for archive producers.
