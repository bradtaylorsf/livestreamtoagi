## Patterns
- Splitting the form into `validation.ts` + `state.ts` + per-section components made each piece independently unit-testable without a DOM (vitest in `environment: "node"`).
- Source-string regex assertions on component files (reading `CreatorForm.tsx` via `readFileSync` and grepping for required imports/JSX) are a cheap structural-regression net when the test runner has no jsdom.
- `Suspense` wrapper around `useSearchParams()` consumer is the right Next.js App Router pattern for query-driven preselection.

## Anti-Patterns
- Wiring a redirect query param (`?queued=1`) without also wiring the consumer — acceptance criteria that say "shows a banner" need an end-to-end check, not just a successful redirect.
- Submitting form params (`factions`, `memory_seed`, `energy`, `excluded_agents`) that the backend stores but never reads when launching the runner — the form looks correct but the values are silently dropped until upstream deps land. Worth flagging in the issue rather than masking.
- Source-string regex tests will not catch behavioural breakage (e.g. an onClick handler that no-ops); fine as a structural net but should not be confused with render tests.
