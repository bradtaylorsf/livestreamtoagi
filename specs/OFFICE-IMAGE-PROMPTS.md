# Office Image Prompts

Pixel art image prompts for each desk and common area. All pieces share a unified style so they tile together into a cohesive office when arranged side-by-side.

## Unified Style Guide (include with every prompt)

```
Style: 16-bit pixel art, top-down RPG perspective (3/4 overhead view), 32px tile grid.
Color depth: limited palette, max 32 colors total. Lighting: warm indoor fluorescent
with subtle amber tones. Floor: light grey-blue carpet tiles with subtle grid lines.
Walls: off-white with dark grey baseboard trim. 1px dark outlines on all furniture.
Consistent shadow direction: bottom-right. No characters — furniture and environment only.
Output: PNG, transparent background where walls end, so pieces connect seamlessly.
Edges that border other areas should have open floor with no walls, allowing seamless
tiling with adjacent sections.
```

## Assembly Notes

**Tiling order (left to right, top to bottom):**

| Row | Left | Center-Left | Center-Right | Right |
|-----|------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Top | Vera | Rex | Aurora | Pixel |
| Mid | Sentinel | Meeting Area (spans 2) | Meeting Area | Fork |
| Bot | Grok | Whiteboard (spans 2) | Whiteboard | Coffee/Break |

**Edge rules:**
- Desk areas that share an edge with the common area should have open floor (no walls) on that edge
- Corner desks have walls on their two outer edges
- Side desks have walls on their one outer edge
- All floor tiles use the same carpet pattern for seamless connection
- The meeting area and whiteboard sections span two columns to create the open central space

**Recommended generation order:**
1. Generate the common areas first (meeting area, whiteboard, coffee) to establish the floor/lighting baseline
2. Generate corner desks next (Vera, Pixel, Grok, Coffee) to lock wall style
3. Generate side desks last (Rex, Aurora, Fork, Sentinel) to fill the gaps

**Resolution per tile:**
- Each section: 320x240 pixels (4:3 aspect ratio per tile)
- Full assembled office: 1280x720 pixels (720p, matches stream resolution)
- Alternative: generate each at 640x480 and scale down for crisper detail
