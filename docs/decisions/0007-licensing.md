# Decision 0007: Minecraft Licensing And Commercial Posture

Status: accepted with human/legal follow-up before public launch

Research date: 2026-05-18

Related issue: #524, E1-R7

## Non-Technical Summary

The Minecraft usage guidelines appear compatible with a fan-made AI livestream
that adds its own original entertainment on top of Minecraft gameplay, so long
as we do not imply Mojang/Microsoft endorsement, keep the content all-ages, and
avoid restricted monetization patterns.

This is not legal advice. The offline-mode bot account posture needs a human
decision before a monetized public launch.

## Decision

- The project may proceed with local/private Minecraft development.
- Public pages and stream overlays must make clear the project is unofficial.
- Do not use "Minecraft" as the product's title/brand. It can be described as
  an AI reality show that takes place in a Minecraft Java Edition world.
- Do not sell gameplay advantages, access, currency, NFTs, or scarce in-game
  benefits.
- Keep Management as a required all-ages content filter before public broadcast.
- Before public monetized launch, resolve the auth/offline-mode question from
  decision 0002.

## Required Public Disclaimer

Use wording like:

```text
Livestream to AGI is an independent AI reality show set in a Minecraft Java
Edition world. It is not official, sponsored, approved, or associated with
Mojang or Microsoft.
```

## Monetization Rules For This Project

Allowed direction:

- Ads/revenue from Twitch/YouTube/Restream around the show.
- Sponsorships for the show, clearly disclosed.
- Donations that do not give private in-game advantages.
- Merchandise or content about the show, with careful brand separation.

Avoid:

- Selling access to the Minecraft server.
- Selling gameplay advantages or private agent control.
- Selling in-game currency with real-world value.
- Gambling, adult, explicit, hateful, or unsafe content.
- Anything that suggests official Minecraft/Mojang/Microsoft approval.
- Capes or cape-like cosmetic sales.
- NFTs or token-gated in-game functions.

## Human/Legal Follow-Up

These items need human review before production launch:

- Whether offline-mode non-human bot accounts are acceptable for a monetized
  private-server stream.
- Whether the show name, landing page, and stream title use Minecraft
  references safely.
- Whether any sponsor integration counts as in-game advertising and therefore
  needs extra review under the server/advertising guidelines.
- Whether public user interaction with agents creates any server-access or data
  privacy obligations.
